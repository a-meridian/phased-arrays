Executive Abstract

What this part establishes

What a skeptical leadership reader can safely take

  6. Apply dual-layer causal analysis: Use institutional/material explanations as the default baseline and field/metaphysical models as hypothesis overlays that must add predictive value beyond baseline explanations (Ch 14).

R.5.3 Consolidated Limitations

Measurement limitations:

  • No instrument measures “paradigm attenuation” in decibels; dB estimates are order-of-magnitude analogical translations from documented effect sizes (Ch 15)
  • Harvesting efficiency \(\eta\), coupling coefficients \(M\), and parasitic threshold values are theoretical — no experimental calibration exists (Ch 14)
  • The quarantine thinning time constant \(\tau_{decay}\) is asserted, not derived from underlying dynamics (Ch 15)

Model limitations:

  • The model cannot distinguish between levels of metaphor: whether parasitic entities are literal beings, emergent system dynamics, or useful conceptual models remains undetermined (Ch 14)
  • The protective-vs-parasitic veil distinction is qualitative; no metric cleanly separates them (Ch 15)
  • Deceptive jamming detection risks unfalsifiability — if any contradictory evidence can be labeled “deceptive jamming,” the model becomes unfalsifiable (Ch 15)

Evidence limitations:

  • Evidence quality varies widely: ACE Study data is strong epidemiology [L1]; Monroe’s loosh concept is experiential/subjective; channeled material is unfalsifiable by conventional methods (Ch 14)
  • Alternative explanations exist: civilizational collapse may result from complexity catastrophe, environmental change, or emergent fragility without requiring deliberate intervention (Ch 14)
  • Historical pattern matching (peak coherence vs. collapse) is suggestive, not conclusive: correlation does not establish causation (Ch 14)
  • Selection bias in suppression evidence: dramatic cases are catalogued precisely because they are dramatic; quiet acceptance of paradigm-challenging work is underrepresented (Ch 15)
  • Single-source circularity risk in disclosure narratives: key witnesses may trace to fewer independent sources than they appear (Ch 15)
